How Dangerous is Fentanyl?

Fentanyl is a highly potent synthetic opiod. But how dangerous is it really?

Opioids


Opioids are substances derived from, or mimicking, the leaves of the Poppy plant. Morphine has been used for nearly a century, and heroin is known for its incredible addictiveness, but in recent decades, fentanyl has come into use.

How Fentanyl Enters the US


Precursor chemicals are manufactured in China, which are shipped to Mexico. From there, the fentanyl is synthesized in drug labs, and shipped by the Sinola Cartel into the US; where it is distributed.

Fentanyl Addiction


Fentanyl is by far the strongest opioid available. It is 50-100 times stronger than other opioids, and is incredibly addictive. By stimulating the pleasure center of the brain much more than can happen naturally, it creates an incredibly strong addiction to the substance.

Effects of Fentanyl


Fentanyl can be prescribed as a painkiller, but is also often taken illegally; due to the euphoric high it produces. But it can also cause many other effects, including itching, sweating, and vomiting. furthermore, if taken often or in high doses, it can induce hypertension, hallucinations, delirium, and overdose.

Fentanyl Overdose


It takes only two milligrams of fentanyl to cause an overdose. This causes breathing and other bodily facilities to slow down, and if they slow down too much, death follows. This is called Hypoxia.

Narcan


If someone is overdosing on fentanyl, it can take less than ten minutes for them to suffocate, but the medication nalaxone (Narcan) can tempoarily pause the overdose, giving EMS time to arrive and rescue them.
